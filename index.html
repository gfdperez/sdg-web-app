What is sustainable development goals?

17 Goals To A BETTER World

The Sustainable Development goals are the blueprints to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all. Providing a focus for the international community’s development efforts until 2030, it serves as a yardstick by which progress will be measured. It is has become a universal plan for all countries to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure prosperity for everyone as they are intended to be tackled as a group rather than individually.

How did it started?

The 2012 Rio+ Summit, held in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, lead to the creation of the United Nations Conference on Sustainable Development. There they then created the Sustainable Development Goals with the purposed to create a set of global goals, related with the environmental, political and economic challenges that we face as humanity in 2013. The group involved members from 70 countries and published its final draft in July 2014.

last 10 years to deliver the goals

With 10 years remaining until 2030, the decade of action is a need to usher in a decade of ambitious action to deliver the Goals by the proposed date. It is a hopeful call to action to mobilize everyone, everywhere, to demand urgency and ambition to be able to end extreme poverty, and to shine a light on solutions that expand access and demonstrate the possibilities of ideas by driving sustainable innovation, financial investments and technology—while making space in our communities and cities for young people to lead.
